Cryptocurrencies:
The Digital Revolution

They're everywhere—on TV, backed by celebrities, and transforming how we think about money. Let's break down the fundamentals.

Start Learning

1. The Core Definition

Cryptocurrencies, or "crypto," are a fresh type of digital cash that uses technology to operate without oversight from governments or banksNo central authority controls supply or transactions. They represent a global, fully digital, and open-access form of money.

2. Crypto vs. Fiat Money

How do they stack up against regular money, known as "fiat"Government-issued currency like USD, EUR (like the US dollar or Euro)? Fiat money is handled by a central authority (the Federal Reserve, ECB). Crypto skips this step entirely.

Key Attributes

Click any card to learn more

💻

Fully Digital

There are no paper notes or coins. It's all code running on computers, making it a purely digital asset.

Since crypto exists only as data on a distributed network, it can never be physically seized, lost in a fire, or worn down over time—unlike paper bills or metal coins.
🌐

Borderless Transfers

You can send crypto across the globe in seconds, ignoring distances or national lines. All you need is a device and internet.

Traditional wire transfers can take days and cost significant fees. Crypto transactions typically settle in minutes regardless of geographic distance.
🔓

Open to Anyone

Sending or receiving crypto doesn't require signing up or jumping through hoops. You skip the middleman.

Over 1.4 billion adults worldwide are unbanked. Crypto offers a financial system accessible with only a smartphone and internet connection.
🛡️

Some Privacy

Instead of personal details, you use a random string called an "address." Your real name is not linked.

While transactions are pseudonymous, they are also publicly recorded on the blockchain—meaning they are traceable with the right tools.
⚖️

Decentralized Power

No single authority calls the shots. Decentralization prevents any government or bank from interfering.

Thousands of independent nodes worldwide verify each transaction, making censorship or shutdown of the network practically impossible.
🧠

Created by Code

Units are created following rules baked into software code. Supply follows code rules, not a bank's whims.

Bitcoin, for example, has a hard cap of 21 million coins enforced by its code—no person or authority can override this rule.

How Supply and Security Work

📊 The Supply Factor

Supply plays a big role in how valuable a crypto is. Based on the code:

  • ▸ Some cryptocurrencies have a hard limit on total supply, which builds in rarity.
  • ▸ Others have no cap, though they may restrict the annual issuance rate.

🔐 The "Crypto" in Cryptocurrency

They are built to resist fakes. This is where the crypto part comes in:

  • ▸ Cryptography uses math to keep transactions secure and logged properly.
  • ▸ It means hiding info through mathematical tricks—like Caesar's cipher, but now digital.
  • ▸ The safety feature relies on immutable recording of all transactions.
✨

Summary: Why Crypto is Unique

All these features—being fully digital, borderless, peer-to-peer, code-driven, and fake-proof— could allow people to handle their money without outsiders butting in. While the full impact is still evolving, cryptocurrencies are clearly gaining traction as a new kind of investment and financial tool.
