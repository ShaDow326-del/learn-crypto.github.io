1. The Core Definition

Cryptocurrencies, or "crypto," are a fresh type of digital cash that uses technology to operate without oversight from governments or banks. They represent a global, fully digital, and open-access form of money.

2. Crypto vs. Fiat Money

How do they stack up against regular money, known as "fiat" (like the US dollar or Euro)? Fiat money is handled by a central authority (the Federal Reserve, ECB). Crypto skips this step entirely.

How Supply and Security Work

The Supply Factor

The supply plays a big role in how valuable a crypto is. Based on the code:

  • Some cryptocurrencies have a hard limit on how many will ever exist, which builds in rarity.
  • Others have no cap, though they might restrict how many new ones pop up each year.

The "Crypto" in Cryptocurrency

They are built to resist fakes. This is where the crypto part comes in:

  • **Cryptography** uses math to keep transactions secure and logged properly.
  • It means hiding info through writing tricks (like Caesar used, but now digital).
  • The safety feature relies on smart recording of all transactions.
